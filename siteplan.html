Art in the City — Site Plan

Site plan describing purpose, audience scenarios, visual design, typography, and wireframes for an urban art discovery and community platform.

Site Name

Art in the City

Why this name: "Art in the City" clearly communicates the focus on urban public art and makes the site discoverable for city residents and visitors seeking murals, installations, and street art. It's broad enough to include maps, artist profiles, events, and a virtual gallery.

Optional domain availability (example): artinthecity.org

Site Purpose

Art in the City is a digital platform for urban art enthusiasts. It provides a centralized, interactive experience for discovering and engaging with public art in the city. Key content and services include:

  • An interactive map of urban art locations (murals, sculptures, installations) with filters and directions.
  • Artist profiles and portfolios showcasing work and contact/social links.
  • A blog with news, interviews, and features about local art and artists.
  • A community forum for discussion, meetups, and collaboration.
  • A virtual gallery to view high-resolution images and virtual exhibitions.
  • Event listings for festivals, gallery openings, and public art tours.

User Scenarios

Representative questions visitors might ask:

  1. "Where is the nearest mural that features portrait work and is wheelchair accessible?"
  2. "How can I find an artist's profile and contact them about a collaboration or commission?"
  3. "How do I submit my photo of a mural or request my artwork be included in the virtual gallery?"

Color Schema

Selected palette (used exclusively on this document):

  • Primary — Cascade Green #0B6B4F (used for headings, primary buttons, and key accents)
  • Accent — Sunset Coral #FF6B6B (used for links, call-to-action highlights, icons)
  • Neutral — Soft Fog #F6F7F5 (page background and subtle panel backgrounds)

Usage examples: headings use Cascade Green, primary CTAs and important badges use Sunset Coral, body text sits on Soft Fog background with dark neutral text for contrast.

Typography

Google Fonts selected (2 fonts):

  • Montserrat — used for headings, navigation, and buttons (clear geometric sans for titles and UI).
  • Lora — used for body copy and long-form content (serif for comfortable reading and a slightly traditional outdoor feel).

Font sizes examples in implementation: H1 — 2rem (Montserrat 700), H2 — 1.25rem (Montserrat 700), body — 1rem (Lora 400).

Implementation Notes

  • Interactive map: integrate a mapping library (Leaflet, Mapbox) and geotagged entries with photos and artist links.
  • Profiles & moderation: artist profiles should allow social links and a moderation workflow for user-submitted content.
  • Accessibility: provide high-contrast options, descriptive alt text for artwork, keyboard navigation, and captioning for any video interviews.
  • Responsive: mobile-first layout with a switchable map/list view for small screens.
