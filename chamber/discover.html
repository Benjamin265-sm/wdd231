Lake Malawi National Park

Located at the southern end of the great expanse of Lake Malawi, with its deep, clear waters and mountain backdrop, the national park is home to many hundreds of fish species, nearly all endemic. Its importance for the study of evolution is comparable to that of the finches of the Galapagos Islands.

Mulanje Mountain Forest Reserve

Mulanje Mountain is a massive granite inselberg in southern Malawi. It is the highest point in Malawi and the second highest in Central Africa after Mount Stanley in Uganda and the Democratic Republic of Congo.

Read More

Liwonde National Park

Liwonde National Park, with incredible wildlife reintroductions and translocations, has become a haven for wildlife and a valuable asset for Malawi and its people.

Read More

Zomba Plateau

Zomba Plateau is a highland retreat in southern Malawi, known for its cool climate, scenic walking trails, and stunning views. Activities include hiking, horse riding, and birdwatching.

Read More

Nyika National Park

Nyika National Park, located in northern Malawi, is the country's largest and most unique wildlife reserve, characterized by rolling grassy highlands that resemble European meadows. It supports large herds of zebra, roan antelope, and eland, and is a birder’s paradise.

Read More

Likoma Island

Likoma Island, situated in northern Lake Malawi, is a tranquil and culturally rich destination. Visitors can snorkel, explore local villages, or relax in eco-lodges.

Read More

Chongoni Rock Art Area

Chongoni Rock Art Area, located in the Dedza Highlands, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site featuring rock shelters with ancient paintings by the BaTwa and Chewa people.

Read More

Namalikhate Lodge and Kungoni Centre for Culture and Art

Namalikhate Lodge and Kungoni Centre for Culture and Art is a cultural attraction dedicated to preserving local traditions, housing a museum with woodcarvings, traditional masks, and artifacts.
